About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born physicist, Nobel Prize winner, and one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. He created the theories of relativity, contributed to quantum theory, and explained phenomena such as the photoelectric effect and Brownian motion.


Important Events in Einstein's Life

  1. 1879 – Born in Ulm, Germany
  2. 1905 – Published the Special Theory of Relativity
  3. 1921 – Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics
  4. 1933 – Emigrated to the USA
  5. 1955 – Died in Princeton, USA

Key Contributions and Inventions

  • Theory of Special Relativity
  • General Theory of Relativity
  • Explanation of the Photoelectric Effect
  • Brownian Motion Theory
